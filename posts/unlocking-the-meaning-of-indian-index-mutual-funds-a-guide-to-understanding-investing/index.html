1. What is an Indian Index Mutual Fund?#

An Indian Index Mutual Fund is a type of investment fund that tracks an Indian stock market index, such as the Nifty50 or the BSE Sensex. The fund manager will choose the stocks that make up the index and will aim to mimic the performance of the index. This allows investors to spread their risk over a large basket of stocks and minimise their exposure to individual stock volatility. As the fund passively tracks the index, it provides greater returns than mutual funds that actively select stocks. Furthermore, performance fees are usually lower than actively managed funds. However, investors should be aware that the performance of the fund is closely correlated to the performance of the index.

Q3: What is the difference between an index mutual fund and an ETF? A3: The main difference between an index mutual fund and an ETF is how each is traded and held. An index mutual fund is bought and sold through the fund itself, and the fund is held in the investor’s brokerage account. An ETF is traded directly on the stock exchange and is held in the investor’s brokerage account.

A4: The risks associated with investing in an index mutual fund are similar to those associated with investing in any other type of mutual fund.
